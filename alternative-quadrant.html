Alternative Design: Quadrant Chart

OECD countries categorized into four groups based on median life expectancy and health spending levels.

Design Rationale

This alternative groups countries into four categories based on whether they fall above or below the median values for life expectancy and health spending. This categorical approach simplifies interpretation but sacrifices precision.

Strengths

  • Simple categorical grouping - easy to understand
  • Highlights "efficiency" patterns (high outcomes despite low spending)
  • Good for strategic insights and policy discussions
  • Less overwhelming than continuous scales
  • Clear identification of outlier groups

Limitations

  • Loss of precision - continuous data becomes categorical
  • Arbitrary threshold decisions (why use median?)
  • Can't see fine-grained differences within quadrants
  • Countries near boundaries appear very different despite small actual differences
  • Doesn't show strength of relationship or trends
