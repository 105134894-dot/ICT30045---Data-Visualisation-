Alternative Design: Geographic Bubble Map

OECD countries positioned geographically with bubble size representing health spending and color representing life expectancy.

Design Rationale

This alternative uses geographic positioning to place countries on a simplified world map. Bubble size encodes health spending (% GDP), and color encodes life expectancy. This approach leverages spatial familiarity but may not be ideal for analyzing relationships.

Strengths

  • Leverages geographic knowledge - people know where countries are
  • Engaging visual - maps naturally draw attention
  • Can identify regional patterns or clusters
  • Intuitive for general audiences

Limitations

  • Geographic position is irrelevant to the spending/expectancy relationship
  • Overlapping bubbles in Europe (many OECD countries close together)
  • Hard to compare exact values between distant countries
  • Doesn't clearly show correlation between variables
  • Requires more complex implementation
